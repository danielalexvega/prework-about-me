Introduction

Hello! My name is Daniel Vega. I'm a high school math and computer science teacher. I have taught for 7 years, and I currently teach at McCallum High School, which is the fine arts magnet for Austin ISD.

Where I'm From

I'm originally from Houston, Texas. I moved to San Marcos in 2005 to attend Texas State University, where I majored in Sound Recording Technology. I moved to Austin in 2011, and started teaching in 2013 at the Alternative Learning Center, which is the Discipline High School. I taught there for the first four years of my career, before moving to McCallum.

What are your favorite hobbies?

My favorite hobby is making music. I've been in several bands since college, and have put out four records and three EPs, give or take. I play the drums, guitar, bass, keyboards, and sing. I enjoy all genres, and really love focusing on the problem-solving aspect of music writing. Every riff or chord progression is a riddle waiting to be solved, and I can't stop thinking about it until I've recorded the song. The bands I've been in are Zlam Dunk, Tactics, Desert Culture, Crooked Numbers, and a few others. We've played shows all around Austin and Texas, including Houston, Dallas, and San Antonio. I've also been on a few tours, playing Chicago, New York City, Boston, Philidelphia, Washington D.C, and most cities on the East Coast. You can listen to my latest record by Crooked Numbers here. My other favorite hobby is watching and playing sports. I love the NBA, and I love playing ultimate frisbee.

What's your dream job?

I'm not sure what my dream job would be; I think every job has it's pros and cons. I would love to make music full time, but then I might have to make music with the goal of making money, and I prefer to just write whatever I want without the need to sell anything. I loved being a teacher; it's just a sad reality that they're not paid what they're worth. My dream job then would be somewhere that I can learn and solve problems. I think I will find that in a backend developer role, although I'm sure I'll have a better idea after I finish this bootcamp.

Where do you live?

I currently live in the Mueller neighbor hood, on the East side of Austin, about 3 miles from the University of Texas.

Why do you want to be a web developer?

I taught Math and AP Computer Science, and I love the problem solving nature of both subjects. I believe I can get that satisfaction as a developer.
